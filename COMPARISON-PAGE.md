### Section 3: The Middleman Problem (≈250 words)

**H2: What you're actually paying for on Angi, TaskRabbit & Thumbtack**

These platforms make money by connecting you with contractors — and that connection has a cost. Here's how the economics work:

- **Angi:** Pros pay lead fees of $15–$85+ per job referral. Those fees get baked into the quote you receive. You're not paying Angi directly, but you are paying for their marketing budget.
- **TaskRabbit:** You pay an hourly rate set by your Tasker, plus a **15% service fee** added at checkout. A $100 job costs you $115 before tip.
- **Thumbtack:** Pros pay per lead or per-contact. Prices tend to be competitive, but you're still filtering through profiles of people who may or may not serve your area.

With Fix It First, you call or text a number. Ruben gives you a price. That's it. No service fee, no algorithm, no waiting 48 hours for a match.

**H3: Why that matters for small jobs in Charlotte County**

Platforms are optimized for higher-ticket jobs. If your screen door needs rehanging or you've got a drywall scuff the size of a golf ball, many platform pros aren't taking those calls — the lead fee alone doesn't make it worth their time.

Ruben takes the small jobs. That's the whole point of Fix It First.
